Goal

A novel <500M-param VLA contribution, publishable Q1–Q3, on a 1×A100-class budget. Standard LIBERO average is saturated (94–99% across 0.5B–7B models) — not a publishable headline anymore. Settled direction: a controlled action-representation atlas — one fixed backbone, 5 action heads, measuring a few-shot data-efficiency crossover (does a compact-output head beat a raw-chunk head at low demo counts?) across LIBERO-Long, LIBERO-Plus, and Meta-World-hard, the axes where models still discriminate.

Architecture

SmolVLM2-500M (507.5M params), frozen, loaded once and shared across all 5 heads. Only the action head trains.

  • ~07-05 (~3h18m follow-up)
    Refined N=100 evaluation
    l1_chunk confirmed real and scaling with demos (8–19%); bspline's one promising cell resolved to noise (3.3% → 0.0%).
  • ~07-06
    Decoder porting
    Replaced placeholder GRU decoders with real frozen-vocab decoding. ar_tokens improved substantially (jerk 79–109 → 0.99–10.5). fast_tokens showed no change — traced to eval teacher-forcing on a dummy zero target instead of real autoregressive generation.
  • 2026-07-07
    fast_tokens decode bug — root-caused, fixed, validated
    Root cause: forward()'s loss never included an EOS training target, so generate() always ran to the 56-token safety cap — feeding physical-intelligence/fast's decode() an over-length sequence silently triggered its all-zero fallback (the frozen mean_jerk=0.0041915... constant seen in every prior run). Fix: train on an appended eos_id target. Confirmed at the token level (EOS hit 10/10, decode succeeds) and at the metric level (re-validation: mean_jerk now 9.07–9.78, no longer frozen; success_rate still 0%, matching ar_tokens' known limitation — not a regression).
  • 2026-07-07 evening
    Frozen-backbone representation bottleneck confirmed — novelty re-checked
    Two cheap no-training diagnostics: cached pooled features are 99.6–99.9% cosine-similar across wildly different robot states (targets differ by ~3 L2), and neither image-token-only pooling nor last-token hidden state fix it — ruling out a pooling bug and confirming a real frozen-backbone information bottleneck. Novelty check: crossing backbone-freezing strategy with action-head type at <500M is not scooped (nearest: arXiv 2605.25802, 2601.03309, 2606.14153, and OpenVLA-OFT 2502.19645 which fixes LoRA as its only adaptation strategy) — this strengthens the paper's contribution.
  • 2026-07-07 night
    Bottleneck pinpointed to the vision encoder, not the LLM decoder
    Per-layer probe across all 32 LlamaModel decoder layers: collapse is already ~1.0000 at layer 0 (right after the vision encoder+connector), and the 32 decoder layers only help (recovering to ~0.997), never hurt. Raw pixels alone are meaningfully different (cos_sim 0.9667) — confirming the frozen vision_model+connector (~98M of the 507M params) throws away most of the signal before the LLM ever sees it.
  • 2026-07-07 late night
    Smoke test v1: naive unfreezing makes collapse worse
    Unfroze vision_model+connector, trained jointly with an l1_chunk head on 22 samples, plain L1 loss: task loss dropped (0.44→0.25) but pooled cos_sim went from 0.9961 to a perfectly-collapsed 1.0000. Classic representation collapse — easier for gradient descent to collapse the encoder and predict the mean target than to actually discriminate frames. v2 added a VICReg-style variance-preservation penalty to fight this directly.
  • 2026-07-08
    v2 hit a resource bug — stuck near-OOM on a6000-left GPU1
    A batch_size=8 update pinned GPU1 at 48.4/49.1GB and made it crawl (~20-30 min/epoch instead of the expected minutes). Confirmed NOT hung after a few checks — var_loss dropping steadily (0.91→0.18), task_loss too (0.52→0.27), never collapsed, never crashed.
  • 2026-07-08
    Fix confirmed: cos_sim 0.9961 → 0.2845
    All 15 epochs completed cleanly. Pooled cosine similarity across the 43 samples went from near-total collapse (0.9961) to genuinely differentiated (0.2845 mean, min -0.0681) — strong evidence both the root-cause diagnosis (vision-encoder collapse) and the fix (unfreeze + variance regularization) are correct. Open question: does this translate to real task success_rate, which needs an actual LIBERO eval, not just cos_sim.
  • 2026-07-08
    Batching bug diagnosis corrected
    Built a genuinely batched version (v3) to fix what looked like a "Python loop vs batched forward" bug — confirmed correct (same BEFORE cos_sim) but hit the same 48GB ceiling once backward passes started. The real lever is batch size itself (activations for N samples × 32 layers must be retained for backward regardless of loop-vs-batched), not the calling pattern. Real experiment needs batch_size 2-4 or gradient checkpointing instead.
  • 2026-07-08
    Open question: is the anti-collapse fix robust run-to-run?
    v3's var_loss isn't declining like v2's did at the same epoch (0.80-0.98 vs 0.33-0.45), same hyperparameters, only the unseeded init/shuffling differs. task_loss still tracking similarly. Not resolved yet — worth multi-seed testing before trusting a single confirmatory run for the real experiment.
  • 2026-07-08
    Real-experiment proposal drafted — awaiting go-ahead
    Vision-encoder+connector unfreeze with variance regularization, 5 heads × {shot10, shot50} on libero_long (10 cells), with a corrected (smaller) batch size. Cost: loses the feature-caching optimization entirely — realistically a full day or more of shared-GPU time. Not launched without explicit sign-off; see handoff.md for the full proposal.

Open decisions

  1. Backbone freezing strategy — diagnostic confirms frozen features are a real bottleneck (99%+ cosine similarity across different robot states). Planned cheapest test: trainable last-K backbone layers on an earlier cached layer, not a full unfreeze. Not yet implemented.
  2. Scope expansion order — fix decoders (done) → validate backbone-adaptation hypothesis (diagnosed, experiment not yet run) → expand task/suite coverage → add ≥3 seeds → scale eval N toward 500/cell.
  3. Modal vs. lab GPUs — lean toward free lab GPUs for bulk work, Modal only for quick/cheap validation (real-money budget).
